Florida Foreclosure: A Homeowner Guide

Key points

  • Foreclosures in Florida go through the courts (judicial).
  • You may sell before the final sale to avoid a forced loss of the home.
  • A direct sale is as‑is. No repairs or showings.
  • Ask a Florida attorney about timelines and rights.

What this means

Florida uses a judicial foreclosure process. You may have options before a final sale, including reinstatement, modification, or selling the home to satisfy the loan.

Every case is unique. Talk with a Florida attorney or HUD‑approved housing counselor for advice. We can connect you with investors who buy as‑is and cover standard seller closing costs.

FAQs

Can I sell my house during foreclosure?

Yes. Many owners sell before the court‑ordered sale to pay the loan or reduce loss.

Will a sale stop foreclosure?

If it closes before the auction and pays the lender, the case can conclude. Ask your attorney.

What fees will I pay?

Our partners typically cover standard seller closing costs. No agent commissions.

How fast can this close?

Often in 1–3 weeks depending on title and payoffs. You choose the date.
